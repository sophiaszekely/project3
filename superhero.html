THE TALE OF LAGOONA
Lagoona is well known for embodying mother nature. She is the goddess of nature, wild animals, neo-pagan magic, and protector from man made disasters. She travels forests in search of danger. This was claimed to be thousands of years ago. Her life is isolated because she lives alone in a Romanian forest because she accidentally killed her family and feels bad about it. In her free time she heals and helps plants and animals to atone for the lives she took. Then the forest fire happened. It was a burst of flames and the fall of every living thing in the forest. It was the biggest loss in the existence of the Romanian forest. Old oaks fell to the ground in ash and the small animals ran in fear of what was soon to be the end of their lives. She leaped to her feet as she sensed flames rising. The air felt dry and the taste of the wind was hot. The feel of the ground on her finger tips was off and she knew there was a fire near by. She glanced around the tree cluttered forest and looked up into the sky. She found a trail of smoke in the space of bright blue to her right and she ran towards it, already in fear of what the damage could be. Running, she dodged tree after tree and rock after rock. Bolting through the vegetation like a cheetah. After running for what seemed like only a minute, she smelled the smoke. Stopping in her tracks she sees through the trees in the distance. The fire looked like it had been caused by some irresponsible campers. Tents were in flames and the sight of trees turning to ash twisted her insides. After starring in panic for too long, she took out of her felt bag a cherry blossom and pressed it into her hand until she felt the moist flower transfer to her fingertips. She then lashed her palm toward the fire and water flushed out of the middle of her palm. You could compare the sight to a geiser putting out a small match. The fire was soon put out but the damage was done. Rabbits houses were destroyed and once beautiful pinesaps were now light grey dust. The damage had also been done to Lagoona. The plants she had watched grow for so long were gone and she wept. Falling to her knees, she sobbed into her hands. The tears forming pools in her palms and some drops escaping the cracks in her fingers. The tears dripped onto the flowers and grass. As the dust soaked them up, green sprouts rose from the burnt dirt. Growing and growing, the plants touched Lagoonas hands and she removed them from her face to look at what her tears had done. In amazement she had realized that water is life. She cupped the water in her hands and poured in onto the rest of the soil and at the base of the burnt trees. The trees bark would grow brown again and the grass would double in size. The once dead area was now alive again. The rabbits and birds peaked out from afar and ran back into their homes. Standing proud, Lagoona had replenished the piece of the forest where the fire occurred.
